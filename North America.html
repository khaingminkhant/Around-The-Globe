North America is the third-largest continent, stretching from the Arctic in the north to Central America in the south, with a diverse landscape that includes vast plains, deserts, mountain ranges like the Rockies, and the Great Lakes. It comprises 23 countries, with the largest by area and population being the United States, Canada, and Mexico. North America is home to around 600 million people and is known for its mix of cultures, especially due to its rich history of Indigenous cultures and waves of global immigration.

The continent is a major hub for economic power and technological innovation, with global cities like New York, Los Angeles, Toronto, and Mexico City. North America is also known for its natural wonders, including the Grand Canyon, Niagara Falls, and Yellowstone. It is highly developed in terms of infrastructure and industry, although there are regional challenges, such as environmental concerns, income inequality, and social issues.
